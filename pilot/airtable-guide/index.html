Working in Airtable.

A short, practical how-to for following up with people who came to the 5/26 meeting (and everyone who comes after). Use this until the Groundwork dashboard is ready for daily follow-up work. Written for LaNeé, Stephanie, and Kathryn.

The big idea, in 3 lines

  1. Airtable is the source of truth. Don't try to track follow-up in spreadsheets, notes apps, or the dashboard. One place, always.
  2. Filtered Views are your daily work list. You don't browse all 172 contacts. You open YOUR view, which shows YOUR list.
  3. Every call/text/email becomes one new row in the Contact Log table. One row per attempt. That's the discipline.

2Find your list

Set up a saved View once. It becomes your daily home screen. Open Airtable → Contacts table → click "Create new view" → grid view.

Step 1

Name your view

Call it something clear: 📞 My calls — LaNeé or 📞 Kathryn: Amplifier follow-up. The emoji helps you find it instantly.

Step 2

Add filters (the AND list)

For LaNeé and Stephanie (regional follow-up). Click the Filter button at the top. Add:

  • assigned_organizer  is  LaNeé (or Stephanie)
  • attendance_5_26_status  is any of  Attended, Partial

No exclusions needed — Kathryn's filter does the splitting, not yours. You'll see everyone in your region who attended. The Kelley-Coquillette-types (multi-commitment) will be in your list, and you'll handle them as 1:1s.

For Kathryn (narrow training-funnel — Amp/HM only, nothing else):

  • attendance_5_26_status  is any of  Attended, Partial
  • amendment5_commitments  contains  Amplifier  OR  amendment5_commitments  contains  Host house meeting (use the "Add condition group" + "OR" option)
  • amendment5_commitments  does not contain  Parent Power Camp
  • amendment5_commitments  does not contain  Regional launch team
  • amendment5_commitments  does not contain  School board resolution
  • amendment5_commitments  does not contain  Other

The four exclusions keep multi-commitment leaders out — they belong to LaNeé/Stephanie for strategic 1:1s.

Step 3

Sort the list so the urgent stuff is on top

Click Sort. Add:

  • last_attempt_date  ascending  (oldest first — people you haven't touched recently bubble to the top)
  • Then amendment5_commitments  descending  (people who made commitments float above blanks)
Step 4

Hide noise columns

Click "Hide fields" and turn off everything you don't need while calling. Keep visible: Name, phone, email, city, district, amendment5_commitments, last_attempt_date, last_attempt_result, notes.

Pro tip: Bookmark your view's URL in your browser. Airtable view URLs are stable — you can pin it to your phone's home screen and it opens straight to your call list.

3Log a call (or text, or email)

Every touch = one new row in the Contact Log table. This is the durable record. Don't only update the contact card — that overwrites history.

Step 1

Open the contact, scroll to "Contact Log" link field

Expand the contact (click ↗ at the top of the row). You'll see a field called contact_log with a list of previous touches. Click the + Add button next to it.

Step 2

Create a new Contact Log row

Click "Create new record" (don't link to an old one). Fill these fields:

Example: a call where they confirmed attendance for 6/9
date
2026-05-28 (today)
method
Call
result
Confirmed
event
6/9 Emergency Meeting
notes
Talked for 8 min. She'll come 6/9, said her sister might too. Sent Zoom link via text.
contact
(auto-linked)
Step 3

Pick the right "result" — it powers everything else

The result field is the most important. Use these consistently:

  • Confirmed — got a verbal yes. They WILL attend/do the thing.
  • Reminder sent — left a voicemail OR sent a text with no reply. Touch logged, no human contact.
  • No answer — phone rang out, no voicemail option. Try again.
  • Conversation — talked but no commitment yet. Maybe set a follow-up.
  • Cancelled — they explicitly said they can't make it.
  • Do not contact — they asked to stop. Respect it.
Why this matters: Liz's analysis of the 5/26 calls showed that "Confirmed" people turned out at 88%. "Reminder sent" turned out at 28% — basically the same as no contact at all. The result field is how we know what's working.
Step 4 · Required

Update the contact's last_attempt_date + last_attempt_result

This is the step that's easy to skip — don't. Airtable isn't (yet) auto-syncing your Contact Log entry back to the contact row, so until Groundwork is built out, you have to update these two fields by hand.

Quick way: hit Esc to close the log row, then click directly into the date and dropdown on the contact row. Set last_attempt_date to today and pick the matching last_attempt_result.

Why this matters: your view's sort reads last_attempt_date on the contact row, NOT the Contact Log. If you skip this step, the person you just called stays at the top of your list — because as far as the sort knows, you never called them. Your call queue silently becomes useless.

3.5Retire a contact cleanly

For people who aren't going to develop into leaders — whether they politely opted out, only wanted info from the meeting, or actively asked to be left alone. The goal is to remove them from your active call list without deleting them from the base.

Case A — Polite "not for me"

Got what they needed at 5/26, not looking for an ongoing role

They're not hostile, they're just done. Log one final Contact Log row, then take them out of your queue:

Example: someone who came to 5/26 for info only
date
today
method
Call
result
Conversation
notes
Came to 5/26 for info, not looking for an ongoing role right now. Keep in base for future campaign moments.

Then on the contact row, set last_attempt_result = Do not contact. That's the flag that pulls them out of your active recruiting views (all of those filter out Do not contact).

Case B — Active opt-out

They asked you to stop contacting them

Same shape, just with Do not contact on the log row too, so the audit trail is unambiguous:

Example: someone who asked to be removed
date
today
method
Call
result
Do not contact
notes
Asked to be removed from the list. Respect it — no further outreach.

And again, update the contact's last_attempt_result to Do not contact.

Why we don't delete: the whole point of the base is to remember context. If someone calls back in six months around a different campaign moment, you want to know "we already talked to her, here's what she said." Deleting destroys that. Flagging preserves it AND keeps her out of your daily call queue.

4Track a scheduled 1:1

Same pattern as a regular call log, but with one or two extra fields. We don't have a dedicated "1:1" object yet — use this convention until Groundwork has one.

When you schedule it

Log it like any conversation, but be explicit in notes

Example: scheduling a 1:1 with someone who came to 5/26
date
2026-05-28 (date you scheduled it, not date of 1:1)
method
Call
result
Conversation
event
leave blank
notes
1:1 scheduled for Thu June 5 at 7pm CT, Zoom (I'll send link). She wants to talk about resolution at her school board.

The pattern: start the notes with "1:1 scheduled for [day, date, time]". That makes it searchable later — you can filter for "1:1 scheduled" in notes and instantly see all your upcoming 1:1s.

When the 1:1 happens

Log a second row — what came out of it

Example: after the 1:1
date
2026-06-05 (date of the actual 1:1)
method
Other
result
Conversation
event
1:1 meeting
notes
Met for 40 min. She's drafting a board resolution + asked for talking points. Committed to host a house meeting in July. Next step: send her the toolkit + check in next Friday.
Better solution coming: Groundwork will add a dedicated 1:1 type with a date picker and follow-up reminders. Until then, the notes-field convention is the simplest way to keep things visible.

One golden rule: when in doubt, log it. An over-logged contact is fine. An under-logged one is invisible — and we end up double-calling, missing follow-ups, or losing the thread of what was said. Five seconds of typing now saves five minutes of confusion later.
